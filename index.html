About Me

Advancing Rice Improvement
Through Genomics & Computation

Dr. S. Sabarinathan is an Assistant Professor of Genetics and Plant Breeding at the M.S. Swaminathan School of Agriculture, Centurion University of Technology and Management, Gajapati, Odisha. He earned his Ph.D. from Odisha University of Agriculture and Technology in collaboration with ICAR–Central Rice Research Institute.

With more than four years of research experience in nationally funded projects, he has authored over 15 peer-reviewed publications in Scopus/SCI journals, with impact factors reaching up to 6.3. His research integrates advanced genomic, bioinformatic, and molecular approaches to accelerate rice improvement for abiotic stress tolerance.

He is qualified ICAR-NET in Seed Science & Technology (2019) and has contributed chapters to Springer Singapore publications on rice bioinformatics and genomics.

Research Focus

Core Research Areas

🧬
Genomics & Haplotype Breeding
Haplotype-based analysis of key rice genes for abiotic stress tolerance using 3K SNP panel data.
✂️
CRISPR / Genome Editing
CRISPR-Cas9 based editing of OsTPP7 gene for regulating germination under submergence in rice.
📊
QTL Mapping & GWAS
Genome-wide association studies and QTL identification for yield, stress tolerance, and grain quality traits.
🔬
Transcriptomics & Proteomics
Multi-omics approaches to decode molecular mechanisms of crop stress responses and resistance.
🌾
Molecular Breeding
SSR, SNP, and marker-assisted selection (MAS) strategies for accelerated rice variety development.
💻
Bioinformatics & Big Data
Analysis of 3K Rice Genome Project SNP data for population structure, diversity, and breeding applications.
